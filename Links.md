## Design Details 

- Know your audience before you start 
- Know the plot you want before you start 
  - Bar, Scatter, pie.. 
- Start simple 
  - Do the most basic plot 
  - Then add label 
  - Add title... 
- Colors to fit brand and or data
- Highlight data by isolating color or annotating etc if applicable 

--- 
We read left to right 
Top to Bottom 
Attention spans are short 
The upper left corner of you plot will get the attention 
The title will be read then answer looked for 
If you want to arrange data to the right or somewhere else you have to pull the eye to data quickly 

--- 

## Web Design 

Keeping in mind like the above we read left to right and top to bottom 
Navigation is most functional at the top 

You can make an amazing site and break the rules but you also risk loosing the end users. 
- [Great Example of this](https://yesnowww.com/)

The site is amazing and shows creativity and style. However its hard to use, can you find the navigation on the site? Do they know how to design a standard page based on the work section? 

[Apple is usually a gold standard](https://www.apple.com/)
- Clean navigation 
- Scroll animations 
- Put the important stuff to the user first 
- Look at the order they show you information on the products

## Slides 

Many times we are going to have to present our information on slides. We will still follow the formats above as it remains true here. We should always be ethical when presenting our data. However you can use design to your advantage here using what we have learned thus far. We are going to show off our data showing the work performance of the team. They did well this month and saw a lot of increased volume of calls. With that in mind we might show the information like this. 

Lets say that we are reporting call in information from our call center. lets say the data looks something like this. 
- Incoming calls 1,000
- Issues resolved on call 70%
- Issues opened past SLA (Service Level Agreement) 20%

We will imagine the call center took in 20% more calls. Our target for issues resolved on the phone is 40%. However we are 20% over our stated SLA time. We would highlight the wins at the top and just make note of the 20% SLA rate. 


# slide Example

## Call Center Break Down 

- ### Total calls this month **1,000**
- ### This month we saw **20%** more calls
- ### Issues resolved on the call **70%**
    We saw a 20% rate of issues past SLAs

--- 

We put the wins up top and bold the good numbers. We make far less of a deal about the bad stat. We could go a step further and make the good stats a different color too.
